## 1. Sacred Orality at the Mazar

### What is transmitted at the mazar without text
Ziyarat oral tradition is **not about memorizing text** — it is PLT in action:

| Oral form | Content | PLT mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Дуо (prayer) | Formulaic, not semantic | Prosodic shape carries transmission |
| Фотиҳа | Arabic text, Uzbek understanding | Sound pattern = PLT, meaning secondary |
| Ривоят (legend) | Avliyo narrative | Story structure = PLT; specific words vary |
| Йиғи (ritual cry) | Non-verbal | Acoustic PLT — emotion transmission |
| Нола (lament) | Semi-verbal | Melody + grief = PLT channel |

### Otin-oyi as oral tradition operator
The otin-oyi **does not read from a text**. She:
1. Recites from memory (memorized, not written)
2. Adapts formula to occasion
3. Transmits through voice quality, rhythm, breath
4. Teaches by demonstration, not explanation

**This is PLT teaching**: the student learns by being present, not by reading.

## 2. Lullabies (Алла / Alla)

### Alla as PLT carrier
Uzbek lullabies (алла) are sacred orality because:
- Transmitted exclusively mother → child (no written version in traditional practice)
- Contain invocations of God, prophet, saints — theological content encoded in melody
- Pre-Islamic elements (protective address to moon, stars) + Islamic frame = hybrid PLT

### Alla at ziyarat
- Women sing алла at mazar for sick children brought for healing
- Otin-oyi leads алла for women's ziyarat gatherings
- «Алла» formula = condensed baraka transmission

### Key alla formulas with ziyarat content
```
«Алла, алла, болам...» — basic formula
«Авлиёнинг дуоси» — saint's prayer invoked in alla
«Ё пайғамбар, ё хазрат» — prophet/saint addressed
```

**PLT status**: `embodied_oral` — melody shape carries meaning beyond words

## 3. Wedding Songs and Ritual Speech (Ёр-ёр / Lapar)

### Ёр-ёр as PLT
Wedding songs performed at threshold moments:
- Bride's departure from father's house
- Entry into husband's house
- Morning-after ritual

These are **ritual speech acts at thresholds** — the same PLT logic as ziyarat entry formulas.

### Overlap with ziyarat
- Same performers: otin-oyi leads both wedding and ziyarat
- Same threshold logic: departure / arrival / crossing
- Same baraka mechanism: collective utterance → baraka flow

### Lapar (ritual improvisation)
- Semi-competitive female oral composition
- Ziyarat themes: «Мазорга бордим, барака олдим» as lapar motif
- PLT function: improvisation within formula = active PLT generation

## 4. Family Legends (Оилавий Ривоятлар)

### Legend types at ziyarat
| Legend type | Content | PLT function |
|---|---|---|
| Мозор ривояти | How the mazar came to be | Establishes TraceLog node |
| Авлиё карамати | Saint's miracle | Transmits baraka concept |
| Аёл ривояти | Female saint or woman-transmitter | Gender PLT documentation |
| Оила сулоласи | Family line at specific mazar | TraceLog genealogy |

### The genealogical legend
Many families have **a specific mazar** they return to across generations:
- Great-grandmother was healed there
- Grandmother was told to go there in a dream
- Mother went before childbirth
- Daughter now goes

This is **multi-generational PLT**: legend transmission = mazar TraceLog activation.

### Female avliyo legends (documented by Tillyabayeva G.B.)
- **Биби Сешанба** (Tashkent region): healer saint — women bring sick children
- **Момо Хайкал** (Fergana Valley): dream-sender saint — legend: appears in dream to show buried location
- **Фотима Зуҳро мозорлари**: multiple sites across Uzbekistan — legitimized by Prophet's daughter

## 5. Women Storytellers

### Otin-oyi as storyteller
The otin-oyi's pedagogical method is **narrative-based**:
- Does not explain theology abstractly
- Tells stories of avliyo miracles → student draws lesson
- Story = PLT vehicle; theological content = passenger

### Gathering format (Хатм/Маврид majlis)
```
1. Фотиҳа (opening)
2. Қуръон тиловати (recitation)
3. Ривоят / дуо (story + prayer)  ← PLT core
4. Мавлид / Дуои марҳум (for the dead)
5. Назр тарқатиш (ritual food distribution)
6. Дуо / Омин (closing)
```

Each element = PLT transmission layer.

### Storytelling at mazar
- Women gather at mazar → otin-oyi tells avliyo legend
- Others add family variant of same legend
- Collective legend building = TraceLog activation
- **No written script**: pure oral PLT event

## 6. Key Utterances for Corpus Annotation

### Entry formulas (зиёрат кириш формулалари)
```uzbek
«Ё Аллоҳ, зиёратимни қабул айла» 
O God, accept my ziyarat
→ PLT: threshold crossing formula

«Ҳазрати [авлиё исми]нинг руҳига Фотиҳа»
Fatiha to the spirit of [saint's name]
→ PLT: name-invocation activates TraceLog node

«Барака тилаб келдим»
I came seeking baraka
→ PLT: stated intention = UCOMM channel opening
```

### Chilla entry and exit
```uzbek
Кириш: «Чиллага кирдим, Аллоҳ рози бўлсин»
Entry: I entered chilla, may God be pleased

Чиқиш: «Чилладан чиқдим, дуо қилинглар»  
Exit: I completed chilla, please pray for me
→ PLT: communal witnessing = UCOMM transmission
```

### Healing ritual speech
```uzbek
«Дардим кетсин, барака ёрдам берсин»
May my illness leave, may baraka help
→ PLT: spoken wish + spatial proximity = baraka transfer activation
```
