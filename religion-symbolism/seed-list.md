## 1. Pre-Islamic Sacred Worlds

### Zoroastrian layer
- Fire as sacred medium → мозор атрофида чироқ ёқиш (candle lighting at mazar)
- Pure water sites → чашма зиёратгоҳлар (spring shrines)
- Sky/mountain orientation → зиёрат маршрутларида тоғ йўналиши
- Key site: Avesta geography — Хоразм, Бақтрия sacred landscape

### Shamanic / pre-Islamic Turkic layer
- Tree shrines (дарахт зиёратгоҳлари): sacred trees still marked with cloth
- Stone markers: овул тошлари → modern protective object equivalents
- 40-day cycles (чилла): pre-dates Islam, attested in shamanistic practice
- Female specialists: baksi → otin-oyi continuity hypothesis (Lauer 2008)

### Sogdian layer
- Sogdian funerary practice → naos → mazar architectural evolution
- Female figures in Sogdian art: priestesses → avliyo ayollar
- Sacred springs: Навкат, Оқсой (Fergana Valley) — pre-Islamic spring worship → Islamic shrine

**PLT link**: Pre-Islamic spatial orientation and approach ritual preserved through PLT — not conscious religious preservation but embodied practice transmission.

## 2. Buddhist Layer

### Central Asian Buddhist sacred geography (1st–8th c.)
- Stupa → dome of mazar (architectural PLT continuity)
- Circumambulation (tawaf) → Buddhist pradaksina → mazar circling ritual
- Pilgrimage routes: Termiz, Dalvarzintepa → Islamic routes
- Buddha relics → avliyo baraka: parallel logic of sacred body

### Key sites of Buddhist-Islamic continuity
| Buddhist site | Islamic overlay | Current status |
|---|---|---|
| Термиз — Кара-Тепа | Ҳаким ат-Термизий мозори | Active ziyarat site |
| Дальварзинтепа | Қаршибоши зиёратгоҳи | Visited |
| Афросиёб (Самарқанд) | Ибн Бобо мозори | Active |

**PLT note**: Buddhist pilgrimage routes absorbed into Islamic ziyarat — spatial PLT survives change of religious label.

## 3. Islamic Layers

### Sufi-Islamic core
**Tariqat network** (EUO structure):
- Naqshbandiyya (dominant in Uzbekistan): Bahauddin Naqshband complex, Bukhara
- Yasawiyya: Turkestan (Kazakhstan) — crossborder pilgrimage
- Qadiriyya: present in Fergana Valley
- Kubrawiyya: historically Khorezm region

**Wali hagiography** (TraceLog):
- 40+ documented avliyo in Uzbekistan
- Female avliyo: Bibi Seshanba (Tashkent), Robiya Odina (Fergana), Fatima Zohra shrines
- Karamat (miracle) narratives: oral PLT — transmitted by otin-oyi without written text

**Zikr as PLT**:
- Silent zikr (khafi) — Naqshbandi — transmission through gaze and presence
- Vocal zikr (jahri) — sound as PLT, not linguistic content
- Women's zikr gatherings: separate from mosque, otin-oyi led

### Mosque vs. mazar
| Feature | Mosque | Mazar |
|---|---|---|
| Gender | predominantly male | mixed, female-dominant |
| Language | Arabic (formal) | Uzbek/Tajik (vernacular) |
| Transmission | textual | PLT — gesture, presence |
| Soviet period | closed / suppressed | continued covertly |
| NULLO potential | low | **high** |

## 4. Hybrid Continuity

### The layered site
Most active ziyarat sites in Uzbekistan are **triply layered**:

```
Pre-Islamic sacred place (spring, tree, mountain)
    ↓ absorbed
Buddhist shrine (stupa, monastery)
    ↓ absorbed  
Islamic mazar (wali tomb, khanqah)
    = Contemporary ziyarat site
```

**Example**: Чашма-Аюб (Бухоро) — Job's spring (Ayyub/Job narrative) overlaid on pre-Islamic sacred spring → Islamic shrine → active ziyarat with healing ritual (bathing).

### Material hybrid objects
- **Тумор** (amulet): Quranic verses + pre-Islamic protective form → PLT material carrier
- **Дарахт матоси** (tree cloth): Islamic intention + pre-Islamic tree veneration
- **Зиёрат тупроғи** (shrine soil): healing earth — pre-Islamic earth power + Islamic baraka

### Female continuity operators
Women are the **primary hybrid continuity operators** because:
1. They maintained pre-Islamic practices coded as "Islamic" (PLT disguise)
2. Otin-oyi role spans all layers — no strict boundary
3. Domestic ritual space = hybrid space outside institutional control

## 5. Names, Blessings, Mourning, Ritual Speech

### Sacred names at ziyarat
- «Ё Аллоҳ!» — threshold utterance (entry to mazar)
- «Фотиҳа» — Al-Fatiha recitation (universal ziyarat opener)
- «Рухингга Фотиҳа» — "Fatiha to your spirit" (addressing the saint)
- Avliyo's name + «ҳазрати» — honorific address

### Blessing speech (дуо / дуогўйлик)
PLT verbal component — formula, not content, carries transmission:
- «Омин» — collective sealing of prayer
- «Барака топинг» — "May you receive baraka"  
- «Мурод ҳосил бўлсин» — "May your wish be fulfilled"

### Mourning speech
- Crying at mazar as ritual (نوحه / noha): PLT acoustic transmission
- «Йиғладим» — "I cried" as standard ziyarat outcome marker
- Mourning as baraka release mechanism (Sufi interpretation)

### Ritual food (назр / атала)
- Назр — votive offering food (halva, sumalak, osh)
- Prepared by women → distributed at mazar → baraka transfer via food
- **PLT mechanism**: recipe transmission = PLT (not written, body knowledge)

### Textiles at ziyarat
- Белбоғ (belt/sash) tied to sacred tree or tomb railing
- Colour coding: white = purity, green = Islam, red = pre-Islamic protective
- **PLT mechanism**: cloth-tying = non-verbal vow-making
